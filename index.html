Happy Day News — 2026-07-19

First today, a fascinating health breakthrough: Researchers at Texas, USA A&M University have discovered that compounds in coffee may activate a receptor that helps protect cells from stress and inflammation. This could explain coffee's association with healthier aging and lower disease risk, potentially impacting millions of coffee drinkers worldwide.

Over in space, an exciting collaboration: ESA's Mars Express and NASA's Psyche mission recently coordinated observations of Mars, capturing stunning images of the planet's surface. This collaboration not only enhances our understanding of Mars but also helps calibrate the instruments on the Psyche spacecraft.

Meanwhile, a simple environmental win: Beachgoers can help save endangered sea turtles by making three small changes during their visits. Marine biologist Katherine Sayler emphasizes the importance of cleaning up trash, reducing light pollution, and keeping beaches flat to protect nesting turtles along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts.

Next up, a heartwarming community rescue: In Pennsylvania, USA, the Waterville Volunteer Fire Company spent four and a half hours carrying a 100-pound senior dog named Sable down a rugged mountain trail after she became exhausted. Thanks to their dedication, Sable was safely reunited with her owner and is expected to make a full recovery.

Last but not least, a look at community achievements: The latest Global Liveability Index reveals that Copenhagen has retained its title as the world's most liveable city, thanks to its focus on education, infrastructure, and stability. This recognition highlights the positive impact of urban planning on quality of life for residents.

And before we go, let's end on a laugh — here's your daily dad joke: What do you call a bear with no teeth? A gummy bear.
